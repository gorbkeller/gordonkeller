Geometric Algebra Self-study

Sept. 9, 2023, 6:47 am

Initial thoughts

I am, as so many others are, transfixed by Geometric Algebra (GA). It isn't that I truly understand (or can even begin to understand?) the power of this Algebra, nor is it a belief that I will need this sometime in the future. It might be that a part of me recognizes a certain intuitiveness to or profoundness of it all, but I'm unconvinved that that is why I keep finding myself coming back to it. Decoding the significance or usefulness of it, I'm coming to find, is a difficult task for me. Not in the, 'This is hard -- I'll keep working at it,' manner of thinking, but instead the distorted and counter-productive, 'Ow, my brain -- I must be dumb,' flavor.

Motivation

This is why I am now publishing some of my self-study thoughts in this blog. I want to combat the idea that I will never have a command of this material, and the more transparent I am with where I am at in the journey, the easier it might become to have patience in the face of what seem like insurmountable confusion. The self-judgement I harbor around my intellect or capacity to solve problems is insideous and tries to wedge itself between my current state and my potential for growth.
My hope is that by briefly outlining some of my thoughts on the subject matter in a public way, the confidence in my abilities to synthesize complex or unfamiliar topics will be bolstered. Also, if this was a conduit for discussion on the subject matter with someone who stumbles upon this, I would be immensely grateful. There are not many people in my life currently who care to talk about this kind of thing (though sometimes someone will indulge me and let me try out my half-baked notions on them)
